Community

Keep the conversation going after TransparencyCamp by joining one of these communities.

  • TransparencyCamp Google Group

    http://groups.google.com/group/transparencycamp

    A group for TransparencyCamp attendees to keep in touch to discuss specific outcomes that evolved over the course of the event.

  • The Open House/Senate Project Google group

    http://groups.google.com/group/openhouseproject

    This bipartisan collaborative of policy wonks, technologists, government staff, librarians, bloggers advocate for straightforward technological reforms to make access to government information more meaningfully accessible to the public.

  • The Sunlight Labs Google group

    http://groups.google.com/group/sunlightlabs

    Join these civic hackers for public good!

  • Citizens for OpenGov

    http://groups.google.com/group/citizens-for-opengov

    A GoogleGroup for advocates to discuss and strategize the means and purpose of an open government.

  • Government 2.0 Club

    http://www.government20club.org/

    A national organization that brings together leading thinkers from government, academia and industry to share ideas and solutions for leveraging social media tools and Web 2.0 technologies to create a more collaborate, efficient and effective government â€” Government 2.0.

  • GovTrack.us Google group

    http://groups.yahoo.com/group/govtrack/

    An open but moderated mail list for discussions about how to leverage the power of the Internet to share information about government, ultimately to improve the democratic process.

  • Transparency Bloggers

    http://groups.google.com/group/transparency-bloggers

    A space for citizen journalists and bloggers interested in promoting transparency and government 2.0.

  • The US Democracy Online Exchange list

    http://e-democracy.org/us

    A discussion list for those interested in local, state and national e-democracy.

  • Open Knowledge Foundation Open Government list

    http://lists.okfn.org/mailman/listinfo/open-government

    A list for those interested in open government data. For more details see: http://wiki.okfn.org/wg/government
